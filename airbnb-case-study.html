UX/UI // 3 Months // Figma, Jitter

Airbnb App

Airbnb is a global platform for booking short-term accommodations and experiences. Despite its convenience and variety of listings, issues like excessive scrolling, scattered information, and limited personalization can make it harder for users to find suitable stays.

View Prototype

Empathize

Target Audience

Airbnb targets modern, experience-driven travelers who value flexibility, unique stays, and a home-like environment. They seek convenient booking, reliable amenities, and authentic local experiences rather than traditional hotel stays.

User Persona

Sophie is a 24-year-old product designer from Atlanta who travels frequently for inspiration and relaxation. She values stylish, convenient, and well-designed stays near city centers, and she wants a smooth browsing experience with clear information and personalized recommendations to quickly find the right place.

Define

Excessive Scrolling

The existing homepage presents listings in a long vertical feed with randomized content. Users must continuously scroll to explore different stays, and there is no clear categorization or personalization.

Lacks Information Classification

Key information — such as amenities, reviews, house rules, and host details is displayed on one long-scrolling page rather than divided into clear sections. As a result, important content becomes difficult to locate, forcing users to scroll continuously until they find what they need.

Ideate

Solution//01

Enhance the browsing experience by streamlining how users navigate through listings and minimizing effort during exploration.

Solution//02

Organize listing details with clear information hierarchy, enabling users to quickly find what they need without endless vertical scrolling.

Prototype

Horizontal layout and categorized browsing

The homepage was redesigned to include personalized categories such as For You and Popular. A Card Carousel replaced the long vertical feed, allowing users to swipe horizontally and browse more listings with less scrolling.

Tabbed information structure

Listing details were reorganized using a tabbed layout that separates key information like Overview, Amenities, Reviews, and Policies. This structure makes it easier for users to find what they need quickly without endless scrolling.

Interaction Enhancement

While Airbnb’s existing payment process is already straightforward and functional, I saw an opportunity to make it more engaging through motion and micro-interaction. I designed a vertical loop-scrolling payment selector that allows users to smoothly swipe through different types of cards in a continuous motion. When a card is selected, it expands with an animated reveal showing the card information fields and a next button, creating a seamless and visually engaging checkout experience.

Test

Increased browsing efficiency

The redesigned horizontal layout lets users see more listings at once and find what they want faster, making browsing smoother and easier.

Better information accessibility

The tabbed function helps users locate information faster and makes the interface cleaner and easier to use.

Positive user feedback

User testing showed the design felt more intuitive and organized, and my professor praised the payment method interaction as “smooth and enjoyable.”
